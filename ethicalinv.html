How can I invest without causing cruelty

Mutual funds are not an investment option when talking about vegetarian investments.

The clean option to not promote animal cruelty through your financial investments is to directly invest in stocks. I know that may sound tough but that's the only option that gives us complete control to see what happens with your money. There are good helping hands to help us on this path. See the options below

Options

1

Do it yourself

Follow the Beauty Without Cruelty (BWC) investment guide. BWC is an educational charitable trust for animal rights. Periodically it publishes an "Investment Guide" that lists out companies that do not cause cruelty to any animals. If you know how to invest in stocks follow the guide and restrict yourself to the companies marked as green.

Investing in stocks may not be everyone's cup of tea as it is a volatile asset class. Further, not only is it difficult to pick the right stock, timing your entry and exit is also not easy. If this is what you are thinking the next two options should help you

2

Get assistance in equity trading

This option is for those who want to invest in only those companies that are permitted according to the "BWC Investment Guide" but do not have enough financial background to chose the right stocks. Email us for more details.

Personal Finance Advisor

If you want to do investments and not trade in stocks, work with a financial advisor who does "direct equity research" and helps you making the right investment decision. This research is about carefully picking the right companies permitted by the BWC Guide. This option has a "fee" linked to it. You could even start with a small corpus. Email us for find out more.

Would I make money by following this?

Yes, you will. Vegetarian investments are not about low return investments. It is just about restricting the companies that you put your money and the number of companies are not less. There are more than 170 companies from BSE500 that you can choose from. With right financial research you can surely make profit like you would do otherwise.

More importantly you would be satisfied that you are following the principle of "ahimsa" and you could show some reverence for life.
